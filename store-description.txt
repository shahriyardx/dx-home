DxHome replaces the blank new tab page with a clean, minimal dashboard: a clock, a search bar that does more than search, your shortcuts, your tasks, and your reading list — over a wallpaper you choose.

No account. No ads. No paywall. No analytics. Nothing is locked behind a sign-in, because there is nothing to sign in to.

Key Features:

- Minimal & Clean Design: A frosted, layered interface that keeps your wallpaper the hero and stays readable over any photo you throw at it.

- Command Bar: Far more than a search box. Type a question to search the web, a domain to go straight there, or:
    2+3*4 to calculate, and press Enter to copy the answer
    !gh react, !npm zod, !mdn fetch to search GitHub, npm or MDN directly
    :3000 or localhost:5173 to jump to a dev server
  A hint under the bar always tells you what Enter is about to do.

- Dev Servers: Optionally finds the HTTP servers running on your machine and lists them on your new tab, named after each app. Start a project on :3000 and it appears within seconds. Databases never show up — only real HTTP servers. Off until you allow it, and turning it off gives the access straight back.

- Custom Backgrounds: Pick from preset wallpapers in the sidepanel, or right-click any image on the web to set it as your background.

- Bookmarks: Your top sites as clean shortcuts for one-click access. Add, edit and remove your own.

- Tasks: Built-in todo list with deadlines and a calendar view. Right-click any text or link to quickly create a task.

- Reading List: Save pages to read later from the sidepanel or the right-click menu. Mark as read, filter by status, and reach it from both the new tab and the sidepanel.

- Recent Tabs: Automatically tracks your recently closed tabs so you never lose one.

- Clock: Clean time display. Click it for a full-screen focus view.

- Sidepanel: Open it from the extension icon to manage backgrounds, your reading list, and settings.

- Settings: Toggle every section — clock, search, bookmarks, recent tabs, dev servers, tasks, reading list — to see exactly what you want and nothing else.

DxHome is lightweight, fast, and built for people who want their new tab to be minimal, aesthetic, and actually useful.
